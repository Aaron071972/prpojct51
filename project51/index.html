Interesting Facts About Camels

  • Camels can reach 7 feet in height (at the hump) and weigh up to 1500 pounds
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Interesting Facts About Cameleons

  • Almost half of their population lives in Madascar
  • Unlike most lizards, chameleons can't regrow their tails
  • They mainly change their color in order to communicate or regulate their body temperature

Interesting Facts About Pigeons

  • The pigeon can recognize all 26 letters of the English alphabet
  • The fastest pigeon speed was a recorded 92.5 mph
  • Pigeons are highly socialable, often flying with 20-30 flocks at a time

Interesting Facts About Bears

  • Canada is home to nearly 60 % of the world's polar bears.
  • Black bears can run at the speed up to 35mph.
  • Although grizzly bears are large animals, 75% of their diet is comprised of berries, leaves and nuts.

Interesting Facts About Pandas

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown)
  • Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit

Interesting Facts About Cicadas

  • Baby cicadas, nymphs, are the size of rice when they are first born
  • Female cicadas are attracted to the sound of motors
  • They spend years developing into adults before they can emerge to sing, mate and lay eggs
